How to Make a Tofu Veggie Omelet

This omelet definitely packs a punch. With no meat, but plenty of flavor, this is a great way to begin your day.

Food Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup firm silken tofu, drained and gently patted dry
  • 2 tbsp hummus
  • 2 large cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp yeast
  • Salt and black pepper
  • 1/4 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 cup veggies of choice (onion, tomato, mushroom, spinach)

Kitchen Utensils

  • Medium skillet
  • Spatula
  • Oven
  • Oven mitts
  • Food processor
  • Medium mixing bowl
  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F by pressing PREHEAT, 375, and START.
  2. Prep veggies, drain and dry tofu, and mince garlic.
  3. Turn on the stovetop by turning the knob the MEDIUM HIGH.
  4. Heat a medium, oven-safe skillet over medium heat.
  5. Add olive oil and minced garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes or until just lightly golden brown.
  6. Transfer garlic to food processor, along with remaining omelet ingredients (tofu – cornstarch) and mix to combine, scraping down sides as needed.
  7. Add a bit more olive oil and the veggies to the warm skillet and season with salt and pepper and sauté to desired doneness.
  8. Remove skillet from heat and make sure it’s coated with enough oil so the omelet doesn’t stick.
  9. Add back 1/4 of the veggies and spoon on the omelet batter, spreading it gently with a spoon or rubber spatula, being careful not to tear or cause gaps.
  10. Cook over MEDIUM on the stove top for 5 minutes until the edges start to dry.
  11. Fold the omelet in half and place in 375 F oven and bake until dry and deep golden brown – 10-15 minutes.
  12. Using an oven mitt, in the last few minutes of cooking carefully add remaining veggies back on top of the omelet and cook another 1-2 minutes to warm through.
  13. Carefully remove from oven with oven.
  14. Serve with desired toppings.
